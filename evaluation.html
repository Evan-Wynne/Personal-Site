    Evaluation

      What was the outcome of your model?
      I am quite happy with the outcome of my model. I managed to create a model that takes a users specific investment needs into account and gives them tailored statistics which would in my opinion help them to make a much more informed decision with regards to long term investing. My simulation runs 100,000 times which creates an accurate overview of what investment outcome is most likely to occur. I took in the following data points from the user:
  1. Number of years investing

  2. Initial Investment

  3. Monthly Investment

  4. Expected annual return

  5. Expected standard deviation from mean annual return


      I gave the user the following Information after the 100,000 simulations had finished:
  1. Returned all their previous inputted data so they can refer to it during analysis
  2. Average final value of their investment
  3. Median value of their investment out of 100,000 simulations
  4. Minimum and maximum value of their investment
  5. Standard deviation of returns
  6. Probability of losing money
  7. Mean return in Dollars and in percent
  8. A graph of the deviation between the 100,000 results

    Did it act as you had planned?

      My model did go mostly as planned but I had hoped to make it a bit more focused on one asset class. However, I would recommend that this model be used for long-term investing in index funds.

    Does it help solve a complex problem:

      My model solves a complex problem as simulating random investment returns each month for a set amount of years in 100,000 different instances would be impossible by hand. My model allows the user to gain insight into the probability of different investment outcomes in a graphical way with a rather user friendly interface. I would use this model for my own long term investing plans as I also found the probability of using money to be very insightful.

    What were its limitations?

      The limitations of my model are that it does not tailor the investment probability to specific assets. I also think this model should have much more user input to increase accuracy of probability calculations.

    What would you do differently next time?

      Next time I would hope to take in more user inputs and tailor the model to a more specific asset such as a stock. Here I could take in much more data about the company's financials to further increase the accuracy of output.
